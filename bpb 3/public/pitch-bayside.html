The pattern

Right now, Bayside's design team is flying blind after every proposal goes out.

Every designer runs their own follow-up playbook. The craft is strong. The system underneath the craft is inconsistent — and that's where deals leak.

Designer-by-designer formats

Emailed PDFs, paper printouts, phone-only conversations. Two homeowners shopping with two Bayside designers get two different experiences.

Pricing from memory

"Was it $14 or $16 a square foot?" Material prices live in PDFs, supplier emails, and head-math. Different designers price the same paver differently.

Zero visibility after sending

Once the proposal goes out, designers can't see if it was opened, re-read, shared, or forgotten about. The instrument panel is dark.

Follow-up by gut feel

"Should I call today or wait a week?" Same-hour response to engaged buyers far outperforms cold cadence. Without signal, the answer is guesswork.

Scattered conversations

Substitutions in texts. Questions in voicemails. Markup in emails. None of it shared with the team. Sick day = stalled deal.

No leadership read

You see closed deals at month-end. You never see the deals that quietly went silent. The most important pipeline question — "which deals are alive?" — has no answer.

Deals don't usually die because Bayside lost a price war.
They die because the homeowner went quiet — or because the price was off — and no one knew.

Bayside Portal makes the invisible visible.

Every measurement, every material, every page view, every return visit, every click, every message — captured. Surfaced to the designer at exactly the right moment.

A note for the design team

Your creative process stays yours.

Bayside Portal isn't a replacement for how your designers think — it's a layer underneath. Bring any drawing in, however you create it. The Portal scales over your drawing, captures the geometry, and walks the homeowner through the result.

01 · By hand

Pencil on paper.

Sketch the project the way you always have — graph paper, scale ruler, hand annotations. Photograph the finished plan, upload, and the Portal builds on top of your drawing.

02 · In CAD

SketchUp, Land F/X, AutoCAD.

Designers working in CAD export a top-down view as PNG or JPG. The Portal accepts any image, applies scale calibration, and pulls real square footage straight from the drawing.

03 · From above

Aerial photo, no drawing needed.

When the homeowner just wants regions measured and priced, pull a Google Earth screenshot and draw regions straight on the satellite view.

The Portal makes the back-end of the proposal more accurate. The creative front-end stays 100% the designer's call.

The deal · plain language

Aligned incentives. No fine print.

01 · The fee

1% of contract value

Phase 1 today: 1% of every deal that closes through Bayside Portal. Phase 2 (the full tech-stack vision): 2% when we agree to expand scope. Either way, fee only applies to closed deals.

02 · No fixed costs

Zero minimum in year one

No setup fees. No per-seat charges. No monthly minimums for the first six months. If Bayside closes zero deals via the Portal, Bayside pays $0.

03 · The designer split

Up to 50% to the designer

Bayside may pass up to half of the Phase 1 fee directly to the designer of record. Creates real comp upside on every closed Portal deal. Split structure is Bayside's call.

04 · Training

30 minutes to certify

Designers complete a short video course + quiz to earn the split. New hires after the start date certify before publishing their first proposal.

05 · The term

12 months, auto-renewing

60 days' notice to non-renew. Either side can exit clean at the end of any term. Bayside has 90 days post-termination to export all data.

06 · The data

Bayside owns it all

All homeowner and deal data is Bayside's property. Exportable any time. McMullen Properties retains only anonymized aggregate rights for product improvement.

07 · IP buyout

Source available at year 3

After 36 months under contract (Phase 1 or Phase 2), Bayside has the option to buy out the Portal's source code outright. Terms TBD — we'll structure it fairly when we get there. Code escrow available from day 1.

08 · Support

4-hour business-day SLA

Direct line to Tim McMullen. Monthly designer office-hours call. Quarterly business review with Bayside leadership.
