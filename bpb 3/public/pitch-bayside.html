The pattern

Right now, Bayside's design team is flying blind after every proposal goes out.

Every designer runs their own follow-up playbook. The craft is strong. The system underneath the craft is inconsistent — and that's where deals leak.

Designer-by-designer formats

Emailed PDFs, paper printouts, phone-only conversations. Two homeowners shopping with two Bayside designers get two different experiences.

Zero visibility after sending

Once the proposal goes out, designers can't see if it was opened, re-read, shared, or forgotten about. The instrument panel is dark.

Follow-up by gut feel

"Should I call today or wait a week?" Same-hour response to engaged buyers far outperforms cold cadence. Without signal, the answer is guesswork.

Scattered conversations

Substitutions in texts. Questions in voicemails. Markup in emails. None of it shared with the team. Sick day = stalled deal.

No leadership read

You see closed deals at month-end. You never see the deals that quietly went silent. The most important pipeline question — "which deals are alive?" — has no answer.

Deals don't usually die because Bayside lost a price war.
They die because the homeowner went quiet — and no one knew when to re-engage.

BPB makes the invisible visible.

Every page view, return visit, click, message, substitution request — captured. Scored. Surfaced to the designer at exactly the right moment.

The deal · plain language

Aligned incentives. No fine print.

01 · The fee

1% of contract value

Applied only to deals that close after being published in BPB and engaged by the homeowner. The where-they-sign (in-portal, DocuSign, paper) doesn't matter.

02 · No fixed costs

Zero minimum in year one

No setup fees. No per-seat charges. No monthly minimums for the first six months. If Bayside closes zero deals via BPB, Bayside pays $0.

03 · The designer split

Up to 50% to the designer

Bayside may pass up to half of the 1% directly to the designer of record. Creates real comp upside on every closed BPB deal. Split structure is Bayside's call.

04 · Training

30 minutes to certify

Designers complete a short video course + quiz to earn the split. New hires after the start date certify before publishing their first proposal.

05 · The term

12 months, auto-renewing

60 days' notice to non-renew. Either side can exit clean at the end of any term. Bayside has 90 days post-termination to export all data.

06 · The data

Bayside owns it all

All homeowner and deal data is Bayside's property. Exportable any time. McMullen retains only anonymized aggregate rights for product improvement.

07 · Continuity

Code escrow available

Optional escrow agreement so if McMullen Properties ever winds down, Bayside ends up owning the platform. Mitigates the fair concern about key-person risk.

08 · Support

4-hour business-day SLA

Direct line to Tim McMullen. Monthly designer office-hours call. Quarterly business review with Bayside leadership.
